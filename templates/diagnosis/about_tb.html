What is Tuberculosis (TB)?

Tuberculosis (TB) is an infectious disease that typically affects the lungs, but it can also affect other parts of the body. It is caused by bacteria called Mycobacterium tuberculosis.

Tuberculosis is spread through the air when a person with active TB disease coughs or sneezes.

Symptoms of TB

  • Coughing that lasts three or more weeks
  • Coughing up blood
  • Chest pain
  • Fatigue
  • Fever
  • Night sweats
  • Chills

How is TB diagnosed?

Doctors use a variety of tests to diagnose TB, including skin tests, blood tests, and chest X-rays.
